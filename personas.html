Lilah Kennedy

  • 18 years old
  • Northeastern University Freshman
  • Originally from Dayton, OH

Goals

  • Get herself and her friends into Boston and back
  • Minimize amount of time spent on public transit

I never realized how easy public transportation is before! Sooo much better than paying for gas every weekend!

Public Transportation Habits

  • No prior experience with public transportation
  • Uses the bus to get to Boston with her friends on weekends
  • Uses Google Maps and NextBus on her phone - Maps for bus/train stations near her destination, NextBus for timing
  • Has a Charlie Card but occasionally forgets it

Needs

  • To feel safe on the train/bus (peace of mind)

Values

  • Having the option to change her plans due to the information her app gives her

Desires

  • A better way to coordinate groups of friends

Scenario: Figuring out how to get herself and her friends to a restaurant in Boston on a Friday night out

  1. Lilah uses Google Maps to find the busses or trains that go near the restaurant
  2. Since a bus stop is closest to the restaurant, Lilah uses NextBus on her phone to figure out when the next bus stops at their dormitory
  3. The bus is full since it’s a Friday night, so Lilah and her friends have to wait until the next one.
  4. Lilah and her friends are meeting some other friends at the restaurant, so she texts them to let them know they will be late.
  5. Eventually they can get on the bus that takes them to the restaurant.

Elise & Henry Carpenter

  • Henry is 72, Elise is 67
  • Retired couple
  • From Huddersfield, England

Goals

  • Minimize walking time
  • Minimize connections
  • Not look like fools in the train station

We’re in Boston because we want to spend this time seeing the world. We don’t want to get lost and look like fools

Public Transportation Habits

  • Familiar with the train/bus systems of many European cities, but never been to the U.S. before
  • Own a smartphone, but prefer to ask real people for directions
  • Prefer the T to the bus (once got lost on the bus and needed to call a taxi)
  • Buy single-day Charlie Tickets

Needs

  • To get on the right train getting in the right direction

Values

  • Not being too proud to ask for help

Desires

  • To know which stations are crowded/have many stairs
  • To have fun and not be preoccupied about getting lost
  • An app that’s easy to learn/doesn’t require much technological knowledge

Scenario: Getting to sight-seeing attractions in Boston

  1. They go to the hotel concierge and ask which T stations are near the attractions
  2. They ask the concierge to write the station names down so they don’t forget
  3. At the T station closest to the hotel,
  4. purchase a day ticket
  5. Henry & Elise consult the system map and figure out which trains they need to take, making special note of any connections
  6. Get on the next train that comes
  7. Consult the system map inside the train to make sure they know when to get off
  8. Exit the train and wait for any connections
  9. Repeat 5-7.

Samantha Granger

  • 35 year old divorced mother of Alex (2) and David (6)
  • Lives in Somerville

Goals

  • Minimize stress (in the form of crowded stations and multiple connections)

Yeah, maybe the T’s not ideal with the kids, but hey, you try finding parking at the Aquarium after sitting in traffic for an hour and a half

Public Transportation Habits

  • Takes her kids into Boston about once a week to go fun places with their dad
  • Usually ends up taking a new route on the T once or twice in a month
  • Uses Google Maps and the MBTA Trip Planner
  • Has a CharlieCard for herself (the kids still ride free), which she sometimes has to add money to at the station

Needs

  • Keep her kids in sight, safe, and entertained during the probable delays

Values

  • Planning ahead
  • Not getting too upset when the bus/train is late - that’s just how it is

Desires

  • Well organized stations

Scenario: Taking her children, Alex and David, to the New England Aquarium to see their father

  1. Sam uses Google Maps to find the station closest to the aquarium
  2. She then uses the MBTA route planner to look at which trains she must take to get there
  3. She wants to minimize time, walking distance, and number of connections, but the planner makes her pick one. She picks “minimize connections”
  4. Uses the MBTA planner to figure out when they should leave home, but Sam leaves ~15 minutes early to give herself time to deal with delays.
  5. Sam waits for the bus - sure enough, it’s running late.
  6. Sam has to entertain her children while they stand outside waiting for the bus
  7. Sam has to transfer from a bus to the Orange line. Getting a stroller down the steps is a pain, but easier than waiting for an attendant to let her use the elevator.
  8. Sam needs to figure out which train to get on while keeping track of her children
  9. People are very friendly and help her on to the T
  10. Exit the T.

Goals

The T is a tool. It is your responsibility to learn how to use it and how to compensate for its problems

Public Transportation Habits

  • Familiar with the MBTA, but new to commuting
  • Has a Charlie Card which he manages online

Needs

  • To be on time to work/meetings

Values

  • Personal punctuality
  • Being in control over his public transit experience

Desires

  • Independence - not being at the mercy of the bus/train’s schedule

Scenario: Getting to Office by 9:00 AM

  1. Peter checks his phone to see if there are any services delays on the line he usually takes and to double check the morning T schedule.
  2. Gets to his local station ~15 minutes early to give himself some margin for error.
  3. Gets on first train, keeping close to the door so he can make a quick exit
  4. Exits train before getting stuck in the crowd
  5. Works his way through morning commuters to get to his connecting train
  6. Gets on and checks his watch to make sure he is on time
  7. Arrives to work early, the model of professionalism
